Airline Customer Satisfaction
Prediction (Neural Network)

Description :

The airline industry has experienced consistent growth in passenger numbers over the years, indicating a strong and increasing demand for air travel.

However, despite this growth, customer satisfaction remains relatively low, with an American Customer Satisfaction Index (ACSI) score of 74 out of 100. This places the airline industry below sectors such as hotels, video streaming services, banking, and apparel. This gap highlights a key challenge: while more customers are using airline services, their overall experience may not fully meet expectations. Since customer satisfaction plays a crucial role in building loyalty and increasing customer lifetime value, airlines are increasingly focusing on understanding customer needs and improving the overall travel experience.

Dataset

8000 rows of data

Result :

This neural network analysis aims to predict airline customer satisfaction using 8,000 data points, with variables such as Age, Arrival Delay, Departure Delay, Flight Time, and Type of Travel. The model achieved an accuracy of 77.10%, indicating a fairly strong performance in predicting customer satisfaction. It performs better in identifying satisfied customers (recall: 88.13%) compared to dissatisfied customers (recall: 65.45%), although predictions for dissatisfied customers are more precise. The most influential factors affecting customer satisfaction are Age, Arrival Delay, and Type of Travel. Older customers tend to be less satisfied, while flight delays significantly contribute to dissatisfaction. Additionally, customer expectations differ between personal and business travel.

Strategy Recommendations:

  • Improve on-time performance by minimizing flight delays and providing compensation when delays occur.
  • Provide special assistance and priority services for older passengers to enhance their travel experience.
  • Develop tailored loyalty programs based on travel type (business vs. personal).
  • Strengthen proactive customer service to identify and support potentially dissatisfied customers early (e.g., via email, SMS, or calls).

Customer Segmentation
using Clustering

Description :

Coffee shop “Brew Haven” aims to improve the effectiveness of its marketing strategy. Currently, promotions are still general, such as mass discounts, which do not appeal to all customers. As a result, marketing budgets are not used efficiently, and the overall customer experience remains less personalized.

To address these challenges, the management plans to implement customer segmentation using data-driven cluster analysis. By identifying distinct customer groups based on their behavior and preferences, Brew Haven can design more targeted marketing strategies, improve customer engagement, and deliver a more personalized experience.

Result :

Our main target segment is Cluster 2, known as Premium Experience Seekers. This group represents the most valuable customers, as they combine high visit frequency with high spending, making a strong contribution to overall revenue. They also have the highest income levels and are less sensitive to price, allowing the business to apply more flexible pricing strategies. In addition, their high digital engagement makes them easy to reach through platforms such as mobile apps, social media, and digital loyalty programs.

From a behavioral perspective, this segment prioritizes ambience and overall dining experience, focusing on quality, comfort, and premium service rather than low prices. This creates an opportunity for the business to enhance customer experience through better store atmosphere, personalized service, and exclusive loyalty programs. With the right approach, such as personalized promotions and experiential marketing, this segment can be retained and further developed through upselling and cross-selling, making it the most strategic segment for long-term growth.

Key Findings & Analysis

Model Performance

The model yields a Squared Correlation (R²) of 0.634, indicating that approximately 63% of the variance in revenue can be explained by the included variables. While this suggests a moderately strong relationship and a good starting point for predictive modeling, the Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) stands at 68,014.

When compared to the Average Revenue of 180,622, the error margin remains relatively high. This suggests that while the model captures the general trend, it still possesses a high probability of significant individual prediction errors, likely due to unobserved factors or high volatility in sales data.

Primary Revenue Drivers

Based on the coefficients, the most influential factors driving revenue are Bestseller status and Discount levels. Specifically, the "Bestseller = No" attribute shows a strong negative coefficient, confirming that bestseller status is a critical prerequisite for high-revenue products.

Discounts also play a significant role in transaction volume, whereas Ratings were found to have a negligible impact on revenue in this specific dataset. This implies that customers at ShopSmart may be more "price-sensitive" (driven by deals and popularity) rather than "quality-sensitive" (driven by peer reviews).

Strategic Recommendation:

"To optimize revenue, the marketing team should focus on aggressive discount layering for non-bestseller items to clear inventory, while maintaining the 'Bestseller' badge visibility as it acts as the strongest psychological trigger for purchase."
